## DEVELOPMENTAL MILESTONES (Ch 1)
### AAP/CDC Milestone Updates (2022-2024)
- Revised milestones identify behaviors 75%+ of children exhibit at a given age (shifted from 50th percentile)
- 216 milestones across 10 checklists → 159 milestones across 12 checklists
- Added checklists for ages 15 and 30 months (now covers every well-child visit 2mo-5yr)
- ~60% original milestones retained, ~40% new based on research
- Removed vague language ("may," "begins")
- Added social/emotional milestones (e.g., "smiles on their own to get attention" at 4mo)
- AAP recommends ASD screening at 18 and 24 months (reaffirmed April 2024)
- Source: [AAP News](https://publications.aap.org/aapnews/news/19554/)
- Source: [Pediatrics 2022](https://publications.aap.org/pediatrics/article/149/3/e2021052138/)

## CEREBRAL PALSY (Ch 2)
### Novak et al. 2017 Early Detection Consensus
- Published: JAMA Pediatrics, September 2017
- Recommends: GMA (General Movements Assessment) + MRI + HINE before 5 months corrected age
- "Absent fidgety" on GMA + abnormal MRI = >95% accuracy for CP prediction
- Combined GMA + MRI + HINE: Sensitivity 97.86%, Specificity 99.22%, PPV 98.56%, NPV 98.84%
- Source: [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28715518/)

### AACPDM Hip Surveillance Care Pathway
- Hip surveillance = "monitoring and identifying critical early indicators of hip displacement"
- Based on age + GMFCS level
- Pelvis radiograph by age 2 for GMFCS IV-V
- Migration percentage >30% → orthopedic referral
- International consensus modeled on Swedish CPUP program
- Source: [AACPDM](https://www.aacpdm.org/publications/care-pathways/hip-surveillance-in-cerebral-palsy)

### Selective Dorsal Rhizotomy Long-Term Outcomes
- 20-28 year follow-up study (n=95, mean 24.3 years post-SDR)
- Durable reduction in spasticity maintained >10 years
- Motor improvements: sustained in GMFCS I, attenuated in GMFCS II-III, not sustained in GMFCS IV-V
- Better gait and mobility 5-10 years post-op vs intensive PT alone
- Quality of life improvements across most CP-QoL domains
- Moderate evidence for positive influence on ICF body structure/function; no evidence for ICF activity/participation
- Source: [PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5473717/)

## SPINA BIFIDA (Ch 3)
### MOMS Trial (Management of Myelomeningocele Study)
- Design: RCT, N=183, NEJM 2011
- Prenatal vs postnatal repair of myelomeningocele
- Key findings: Prenatal surgery reduced need for shunting, improved motor outcomes at 30 months
- Associated with maternal and fetal risks
- MOMS2 follow-up: Significant physical and emotional benefits at school age (decade later)
- Composite primary outcome significantly better in prenatal group
- No mortality difference between groups
- Results should NOT be generalized to less experienced centers or patients not meeting eligibility criteria (BMI >35 excluded)
- Source: [NEJM](https://www.nejm.org/doi/full/10.1056/NEJMoa1014379)
- Source: [CHOP MOMS2](https://www.chop.edu/centers-programs/center-fetal-diagnosis-and-treatment/moms-2-follow-management-myelomeningocele-study)

## NEUROMUSCULAR (Ch 4)
### Birnkrant DMD Care Considerations (Lancet Neurology 2018)
- 3-part series in Lancet Neurology, 2018
- Part 1: Diagnosis, neuromuscular, rehabilitation, endocrine, GI/nutritional management
- Part 2: Respiratory, cardiac, bone health, orthopaedic management
- Part 3: Primary care, emergency management, psychosocial care, transitions
- Addresses effects of long-term glucocorticoid use on DMD natural history
- Includes adrenal insufficiency management (potentially life-threatening complication)
- Source: [PubMed Part 1](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/29395989/)
- Source: [PubMed Part 2](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/29395990/)
- Source: [PubMed Part 3](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/29398641/)

### SMA Gene Therapy Updates (2024-2025)
- **Meta-analysis (2024)**: 57 studies — Onasemnogene 95% survival, Risdiplam 86%, Nusinersen 60%
- **STEER Trial (Phase III)**: Intrathecal onasemnogene → 2.39-point HFMSE improvement vs 0.51 sham (statistically significant)
- **STRENGTH Trial (Phase IIIb)**: 27 patients aged 2-18, previously on nusinersen/risdiplam → 1.05 HFMSE improvement over 52 weeks
- **Safety**: Elevated liver enzymes in every patient (days 5-7), thrombocytopenia in 32.5%
- **Sequential therapy**: Switching from nusinersen/risdiplam to onasemnogene shows modest but clinically meaningful motor gains
- **Key finding**: Early treatment + high baseline function = best outcomes; improved motor but no change in respiratory/nutritional outcomes
- Source: [Nature Gene Therapy](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41434-024-00503-8)
- Source: [Nature Medicine](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41591-025-04119-2)

## BRACHIAL PLEXUS (Ch 5)
### Microsurgical Timing Evidence
- **3 months**: Patients operated within 3 months needed NO secondary procedures; if biceps not recovering by 3 months, final result will be poor
- **3-6 month window**: Microsurgery indicated for failure of return of function by 3-6 months
- **Longer delays = worse outcomes**: >1 year delay → ALL patients needed secondary reconstruction
- **Current consensus**: Early intervention (within 3 months) yields best outcomes with fewest secondary procedures
- **Ongoing debate**: Exact timing still not fully settled, but trend toward earlier (3-month) referral
- Source: [PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC2884798/)

## PEDIATRIC TBI (Ch 6)
### PECARN Decision Rules Validation
- Original study: 100% sensitivity for cTBI in <2yr, 96.8% in ≥2yr
- External validation (2014): 100% sensitivity, 55% specificity, 100% NPV
- French multicenter validation (2016): 100% sensitivity, 69.9% specificity, 100% NPV
- Specificity ranges 33-70% across studies (population-dependent)
- Source: [MDCalc](https://www.mdcalc.com/calc/589/pecarn-pediatric-head-injury-trauma-algorithm)

### Amsterdam 2022 Concussion Consensus (6th International Conference)
- Updates Berlin 2016 (5th conference)
- Published June 2023 in BJSM
- **Key change**: Strict rest until symptom resolution is NOT beneficial
- Encourage early return to light-intensity physical activity within first 2 days
- Individualized, sport-specific return-to-sport model (new vs Berlin)
- Improved return-to-school paradigm
- Pediatric-specific recommendations for children 5-12 and adolescents 13-18
- Source: [AAP Pediatrics](https://publications.aap.org/pediatrics/article/153/1/e2023063489/)
- Source: [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37316210/)

## MSK / ORTHOPEDICS (Ch 9)
### BrAIST Trial (Bracing in Adolescent Idiopathic Scoliosis)
- Published: NEJM, October 2013
- Design: Combined randomized (n=116) + preference (n=126) cohorts = 242 patients
- **Stopped early for efficacy of bracing**
- Bracing success: 72% (combined), 75% (randomized) vs 42-48% observation
- **Dose-response**: ≥13 hours/day → up to 90% success rate
- Lowest quartile (0-6 hours/day) → only 42% success (worse than observation threshold)
- First rigorous RCT evidence that bracing reduces curve progression risk
- Brace prescribed: ≥18 hours/day
- Source: [NEJM](https://www.nejm.org/doi/full/10.1056/NEJMoa1307337)
