## 1. GUIDELINES FOUND

### AAP Developmental Surveillance and Screening Guidelines
- **AAP Policy Statement (2020 reaffirmed)**: Developmental surveillance at every well-child visit; standardized screening at 9, 18, and 30 months. ASD-specific screening at 18 and 24 months.
  - Hagan JF, Shaw JS, Duncan PM, eds. Bright Futures: Guidelines for Health Supervision of Infants, Children, and Adolescents. 4th ed. American Academy of Pediatrics; 2017.
  - Lipkin PH, Macias MM; Council on Children with Disabilities. Promoting Optimal Development: Identifying Infants and Young Children with Developmental Disorders Through Developmental Surveillance and Screening. Pediatrics. 2020;145(1):e20193449.

### IDEA (Individuals with Disabilities Education Act)
- **Part C (Birth to 3 years)**: Early intervention services; Individualized Family Service Plan (IFSP); natural environments mandate; states define "developmental delay" (typically >25% delay or >1.5-2 SD below mean in one or more domains).
- **Part B, Section 619 (Ages 3-5)**: Preschool special education; Individualized Education Program (IEP) replaces IFSP at age 3.
- **Part B (Ages 3-21)**: School-based services through public education system.
- Referral: IDEA mandates "Child Find" -- states must identify, locate, and evaluate all children with disabilities.

### WHO Motor Development Study
- WHO Multicentre Growth Reference Study Group. WHO Motor Development Study: Windows of Achievement for Six Gross Motor Development Milestones. Acta Paediatr Suppl. 2006;450:86-95.

## 2. LANDMARK TRIALS & SEMINAL STUDIES

### Denver Developmental Screening Test (DDST / Denver II)
- Frankenburg WK, Dodds J, Archer P, et al. The Denver II: A Major Revision and Restandardization of the Denver Developmental Screening Test. Pediatrics. 1992;89(1):91-97.
- Standardized on 2,096 Colorado children ages birth to 6 years.
- 125 items across 4 domains: gross motor, fine motor-adaptive, language, personal-social.
- Items scored as pass/fail/refuse/no opportunity; results classified as normal, suspect, or untestable.
- **[BOARD]** Sensitivity ~56-83%, specificity ~43-80% depending on population studied. Criticized for low sensitivity, particularly in language domain.
- **[BOARD]** Denver II is a SCREENING tool, not a diagnostic instrument. Does NOT yield a developmental quotient.

### Bayley Scales of Infant and Toddler Development
- Bayley N. Bayley Scales of Infant and Toddler Development. 4th ed. Pearson; 2019 (Bayley-4).
- **[BOARD]** Age range: 1-42 months (16 days to 42 months 15 days).
- **Bayley-4 Scales**: Cognitive, Language (Receptive + Expressive), Motor (Fine + Gross), Social-Emotional, Adaptive Behavior.
- Mean = 100, SD = 15 for composite scores; Mean = 10, SD = 3 for scaled subtest scores.
- **[BOARD]** Bayley is the GOLD STANDARD for developmental assessment in infants/toddlers. It is a DIAGNOSTIC tool (unlike Denver II which is screening).
- Bayley-III (2006) vs Bayley-4 (2019): Bayley-4 updated norms, added digital administration, refined items. Bayley-III was noted to overestimate cognitive scores by ~7 points compared to Bayley-II.

### ASQ-3 (Ages and Stages Questionnaire, 3rd Edition)
- Squires J, Bricker D. Ages & Stages Questionnaires. 3rd ed. Paul H. Brookes Publishing; 2009.
- Parent-completed questionnaire; 21 age-specific forms (1-66 months).
- 5 domains: Communication, Gross Motor, Fine Motor, Problem Solving, Personal-Social.
- 30 items per questionnaire (6 per domain); scored as Yes (10), Sometimes (5), Not Yet (0).
- **[BOARD]** Sensitivity 70-90%, specificity 76-91%. Takes 10-15 minutes for parent to complete, 2-3 minutes to score.
- Cutoff scores: Below cutoff = refer; "monitoring zone" = provide activities and rescreen; above cutoff = development appears on track.

### BINS (Bayley Infant Neurodevelopmental Screener)
- Aylward GP. Bayley Infant Neurodevelopmental Screener. Psychological Corporation; 1995.
- Ages 3-24 months; 6 item sets at 3, 6, 9, 12, 18, 24 months.
- Assesses 4 conceptual areas: basic neurological functions/intactness, receptive functions, expressive functions, cognitive processes.
- Risk categories: low, moderate, high.
- **[BOARD]** Takes ~10-15 minutes. Quick office-based screener. Not as widely used as ASQ-3.

### M-CHAT-R/F (Modified Checklist for Autism in Toddlers, Revised with Follow-Up)
- Robins DL, Casagrande K, Barton M, et al. Validation of the Modified Checklist for Autism in Toddlers, Revised with Follow-Up (M-CHAT-R/F). Pediatrics. 2014;133(1):37-45.
- **[BOARD]** 20-item parent-report screen for ASD; ages 16-30 months.
- Scoring: Low risk (0-2), Medium risk (3-7 -- administer Follow-Up), High risk (8-20 -- refer immediately).
- Follow-Up interview reduces false-positive rate from ~90% to ~50%.
- Sensitivity: ~85-92%; Specificity with Follow-Up: ~95-99%.
- PPV with Follow-Up: 47.5% for ASD specifically; 94.6% for any developmental delay/concern.
- **[BOARD]** AAP recommends M-CHAT-R/F at 18 and 24 months.

## 3. RECENT EVIDENCE (2020-2025)

### Updated AAP Screening Guidelines (2020)
- Lipkin PH, Macias MM. Promoting Optimal Development: Identifying Infants and Young Children with Developmental Disorders Through Developmental Surveillance and Screening. Pediatrics. 2020;145(1):e20193449.
- Reaffirmed developmental screening at 9, 18, 30 months and ASD screening at 18, 24 months.
- Emphasized developmental surveillance at EVERY well-child visit.
- Added emphasis on social determinants of health affecting development.

### Early ASD Detection Advances
- Pierce K, Gazestani V, Bacon E, et al. Evaluation of the Diagnostic Stability of the Early Autism Spectrum Disorder Phenotype in the General Population Starting at 12 Months. JAMA Pediatr. 2019;173(6):578-587.
- Emerging evidence supports reliable ASD diagnosis as early as 12-14 months in specialized centers.
- Eye-tracking technology and machine learning approaches under investigation for earlier detection.

### HINE (Hammersmith Infant Neurological Examination) for Early Detection
- Romeo DM, Ricci D, Brogna C, Mercuri E. Use of the Hammersmith Infant Neurological Examination in infants with cerebral palsy: a critical review of the literature. Dev Med Child Neurol. 2016;58(3):240-245.
- Now increasingly used as part of early developmental assessment battery, not just CP detection.
- Optimal scores by 3 months: >57 (out of 78) = normal; <40 = high risk for CP; <50 = at risk.

### Telehealth Developmental Screening (COVID-19 era)
- Significant expansion of telehealth-based developmental screening during 2020-2022.
- ASQ-3 validated for remote parent-completed administration.
- Bayley-4 added digital/remote administration capabilities.

### PEDS (Parents' Evaluation of Developmental Status)
- Glascoe FP. Parents' Evaluation of Developmental Status (PEDS). Ellsworth & Vandermeer Press; 2013.
- 10-item parent interview; ages 0-8 years.
- Identifies parental concerns across multiple developmental domains.
- Sensitivity 74-79%, Specificity 70-80%.

## 4. EPIDEMIOLOGY DATA

### Developmental Delay Prevalence
- **[BOARD]** Approximately 15-17% of children ages 3-17 have one or more developmental disabilities (CDC, Boyle et al., Pediatrics, 2011).
- Global developmental delay: ~1-3% of children under 5 years.
- Specific learning disabilities: ~5-10% of school-age children.
- Intellectual disability: ~1-3% of the population (mild ~85%, moderate ~10%, severe ~3-4%, profound ~1-2%).

### Autism Spectrum Disorder Prevalence
- **[BOARD]** CDC ADDM Network 2020 data (published 2023): ASD prevalence = 1 in 36 children (2.8%) age 8 years.
  - Maenner MJ, Warren Z, Williams AR, et al. Prevalence and Characteristics of Autism Spectrum Disorder Among Children Aged 8 Years -- Autism and Developmental Disabilities Monitoring Network, 11 Sites, United States, 2020. MMWR Surveill Summ. 2023;72(2):1-14.
- Previous rates: 1 in 44 (2018 data), 1 in 54 (2016 data), 1 in 68 (2012 data).
- Male:Female ratio approximately 3.8:1 (previously reported as 4:1; decreasing as recognition in females improves).
- Median age of diagnosis: ~4.5 years; goal is <3 years.
- Co-occurring intellectual disability: ~31-38% of children with ASD.

### Speech and Language Delay
- Late talkers (18-23 months): ~10-20% of toddlers.
- Persistent language disorders: ~7% of children at kindergarten entry.
- Most late talkers (~50-70%) "catch up" by age 3-4 years, but remain at risk for later reading difficulties.

### Prematurity and Developmental Risk
- **[BOARD]** Extremely preterm (<28 weeks): 25-50% have significant neurodevelopmental impairment.
- Very preterm (28-32 weeks): 10-25% have significant neurodevelopmental impairment.
- Late preterm (34-36 weeks): increased risk compared to term, but majority develop normally.
- Corrected age should be used for developmental assessment until age 2 years (some recommend until age 3 for extremely preterm).

## 5. CLINICAL SCALES & CLASSIFICATIONS

### Gross Motor Milestones (Board-Critical Ages)

| Age | Gross Motor | Fine Motor | Language | Social/Adaptive |
|-----|-------------|------------|----------|-----------------|

| **1 month** | Lifts head briefly in prone, head lag | Hands fisted most of time | Coos begin (vowel sounds) | Social smile emerging (4-6 weeks) |
| **2 months** | Lifts head 45 degrees in prone, less head lag | Hands unfisting, briefly holds rattle | Cooing, social smile | **Social smile** (reliable by 2 months), recognizes parent |
| **3 months** | Lifts head 90 degrees prone, props on forearms, minimal head lag | Hands open at rest, bats at objects, hand regard | Cooing, squealing, laughing | Recognizes parent, anticipatory excitement |
| **4 months** | Lifts head 90 degrees, props on extended arms, rolls prone to supine, **no head lag** | Reaches and grasps (ulnar grasp), brings objects to mouth | Laughing, responds to name turning | Enjoys social interaction, laughs aloud |
| **5 months** | Rolls prone to supine (some), beginning to roll supine to prone | Transfers objects hand to hand (5-6 mo), raking grasp | Babbling begins (consonant sounds), razzing | Stranger awareness beginning |
| **6 months** | Rolls both directions, sits with support (tripod), **sits unsupported briefly** | **Transfers hand to hand**, raking grasp, reaches with one hand | **Babbling** (consonant-vowel combinations: "bababa"), responds to name | **Stranger anxiety** emerging, enjoys peek-a-boo |
| **7 months** | Sits unsupported well, pivots in sitting | Radial-digital grasp | Babbling with variety of consonants | Stranger anxiety more pronounced |
| **8 months** | Gets to sitting independently, early crawling | **Inferior pincer grasp** (8-9 mo), finger feeding | "Dada/mama" nonspecific | Object permanence emerging, looks for dropped toy |
| **9 months** | Crawls well, pulls to stand, cruises | **Pincer grasp refining** (9-10 mo), pokes with index finger | "Dada/mama" nonspecific, understands "no," gestures (waving, pointing) | Separation anxiety peaks (9-18 mo), stranger anxiety |
| **10 months** | Cruises furniture, stands momentarily | Neat pincer grasp, voluntary release developing | "Dada/mama" becoming specific | Plays pat-a-cake, waves bye-bye |
| **12 months** | **Walks independently** (9-15 mo range), stands alone | **Neat/mature pincer grasp**, releases objects voluntarily, 2-block tower | **First true words** (1-3 words), "mama/dada" specific, follows 1-step command with gesture | Gives objects on request, separation anxiety, attachment behaviors |
| **15 months** | Walks well, stoops and recovers, begins to run (stiff-legged), climbs stairs creeping | Builds 2-3 block tower, scribbles spontaneously, feeds with spoon (messy) | **4-6 words**, points to desired objects, follows simple commands | Imitates housework, uses cup, indicates wants by pointing |
| **18 months** | **Runs stiffly**, walks up stairs with hand held (2 feet per step), kicks ball, throws ball overhand | Builds **3-4 block tower**, scribbles, turns pages (2-3 at a time), feeds with spoon (improved) | **10-25 words**, knows body parts, follows 2-step commands, **points to body parts** | Copies household tasks, beginning pretend play, parallel play |
| **2 years (24 months)** | Runs well, walks up/down stairs (2 feet per step), kicks ball, jumps with both feet | Builds **6-7 block tower**, turns pages one at a time, imitates vertical/circular strokes, turns doorknobs | **50+ words, 2-word phrases** ("want milk"), uses pronouns (I, me, you), 50% intelligible to strangers | Parallel play, removes clothing, feeds self well with spoon/fork |
| **2.5 years (30 months)** | Jumps with both feet, walks on tiptoes, beginning to alternate feet on stairs | Builds 8-block tower, imitates horizontal line, holds pencil with fingers (digital grasp) | 200+ words, 2-3 word sentences, uses "I" and "mine," 50-75% intelligible | Knows full name, pretend play expanding |
| **3 years** | **Alternates feet going upstairs**, rides tricycle, stands on one foot briefly (1-2 sec), broad jump | Builds **9-10 block tower**, copies circle, imitates cross, cuts with scissors (snips), **draws a person (head + 1 other part)** | **250-1000 words, 3-word sentences**, knows age/sex/full name, uses plurals/past tense, **75% intelligible** | Group play beginning, shares (with prompting), knows gender, dresses with help |
| **4 years** | **Hops on one foot**, alternates feet downstairs, throws ball overhand, catches large ball | Copies cross (+), draws person with 3+ parts, cuts on line with scissors, buttons large buttons | **Full sentences (4-5 words)**, tells stories, asks "why," knows colors, **100% intelligible** | Cooperative play, imaginative play, has friends, dresses self (except shoelaces) |
| **5 years** | **Skips**, hops on each foot, walks tandem (heel-to-toe), catches bounced ball | Copies triangle and square, draws person with 6+ parts, writes some letters/numbers, ties shoes (5-6 yr) | Fluent speech, 5-6 word sentences, defines words, counts to 10+, knows address | Competitive games, follows rules, understands right/wrong, chooses friends |
| **6 years** | Rides bicycle (training wheels off), skips rope, mature throwing pattern | Copies diamond, writes first name, draws person with 12+ parts, legible handwriting emerging | Reads simple words, grammar mostly correct, 6+ word sentences | Best friends, cooperative play complex, team sports |

### Fine Motor Grasp Development Progression
**[BOARD]** Critical grasp progression:
1. **Reflexive grasp** (0-2 months) -- involuntary palmar grasp reflex
2. **Ulnar palmar grasp** (3-4 months) -- objects held against ulnar palm
3. **Palmar grasp** (4-5 months) -- crude whole-hand grasp
4. **Radial palmar grasp** (5-6 months) -- thumb begins to participate
5. **Radial digital grasp** (7-8 months) -- fingers and thumb oppose, object in fingertips
6. **Inferior/scissors pincer** (8-9 months) -- thumb and index finger crude opposition
7. **Superior/neat pincer** (10-12 months) -- precise tip-to-tip opposition of thumb and index finger
8. **Voluntary release** (12 months) -- controlled letting go of objects

### Drawing Development Progression
**[BOARD]** Critical drawing milestones:
- **12-15 months**: Scribbles spontaneously
- **2 years**: Imitates vertical line, circular stroke
- **2.5 years**: Imitates horizontal line
- **3 years**: Copies circle, imitates cross
- **4 years**: Copies cross (+), draws person (3+ parts)
- **4.5 years**: Copies square
- **5 years**: Copies triangle
- **6 years**: Copies diamond
- Mnemonic: "Circle, Cross, Square, Triangle, Diamond" -- roughly corresponds to ages 3, 4, 4.5, 5, 6

### Block Tower Development
**[BOARD]**:
- **12 months**: 2-block tower
- **15 months**: 2-3 block tower
- **18 months**: 3-4 block tower
- **2 years**: 6-7 block tower
- **2.5 years**: 8 block tower
- **3 years**: 9-10 block tower (bridge of 3 blocks)

### Primitive Reflexes: Emergence and Integration
**[BOARD]** Complete primitive reflex table:

| Reflex | Emergence | Integration (Disappears) | Significance if Persistent |
|--------|-----------|--------------------------|---------------------------|
| **Moro** | Birth (28 weeks gestation) | **3-6 months** | UMN lesion, CP |
| **Palmar grasp** | Birth (28 weeks) | **4-6 months** (must integrate for voluntary grasp) | UMN lesion, CP |
| **Plantar grasp** | Birth (28 weeks) | **9-12 months** (must integrate for standing) | UMN lesion |
| **Rooting** | Birth (28 weeks) | **3-4 months** (awake); persists in sleep longer | Frontal lobe lesion |
| **Sucking** | Birth (28 weeks) | **4-6 months** (transitions to voluntary suck) | Brainstem dysfunction |
| **ATNR (Asymmetric Tonic Neck Reflex)** | Birth-2 weeks | **4-6 months** | CP (obligatory ATNR most concerning); interferes with midline hand function and rolling |
| **STNR (Symmetric Tonic Neck Reflex)** | **4-6 months** | **8-12 months** | Interferes with crawling on hands/knees |
| **TLR (Tonic Labyrinthine Reflex)** | Birth | **6 months** (supine), **6 months** (prone) | CP, vestibular dysfunction |
| **Galant (Trunk Incurvation)** | Birth | **2-6 months** | If persistent, can interfere with sitting |
| **Stepping/Walking** | Birth | **2-3 months** (reappears as voluntary walking ~12 mo) | |
| **Placing** | Birth | **2-3 months** | |
| **Positive support** | Birth | **2-4 months** | Persistent in CP |
| **Landau** | **3-4 months** (emerges) | **12-24 months** | Absent in hypotonic infants, floppy infant |
| **Parachute (anterior)** | **6-9 months** (emerges) | **Persists throughout life** | Asymmetry suggests hemiparesis |
| **Lateral protective (lateral propping)** | **6-8 months** (emerges) | **Persists throughout life** | Absence = impaired sitting balance |
| **Posterior protective** | **9-12 months** (emerges) | **Persists throughout life** | Absence = impaired sitting balance |

**[BOARD]** Key reflex facts:
- **Obligatory ATNR** (held >30 seconds, cannot break out) = ALWAYS pathological at any age.
- Non-obligatory ATNR is normal until 4-6 months.
- **Parachute reflex**: anterior emerges ~6-9 months; does NOT integrate (persists for life). Asymmetry = hemiplegia clue.
- **Moro reflex**: Asymmetry suggests brachial plexus injury (Erb palsy) or clavicle fracture; persistence beyond 6 months = UMN lesion.
- Primitive reflexes are mediated by brainstem; integration occurs as cortex matures.
- Persistence of primitive reflexes = failure of cortical inhibition = hallmark of CP.

### Postural Reactions / Righting Reactions
**[BOARD]**:
- **Head righting (neck on body)**: 1-2 months; allows head alignment with gravity
- **Body righting on body**: 4-6 months; allows segmental rolling
- **Equilibrium reactions**: Emerge ~6 months in prone, 7-8 months in sitting, 12-15 months in standing
- **Protective extension**: Forward 6-7 months, lateral 7-8 months, posterior 9-10 months

### Denver II Domains and Key Items
**[BOARD]** 4 domains:
1. **Gross Motor** (32 items)
2. **Fine Motor-Adaptive** (29 items)
3. **Language** (39 items)
4. **Personal-Social** (25 items)

Interpretation:
- **Normal**: No delays, max 1 caution
- **Suspect**: 1+ delays and/or 2+ cautions
- **Untestable**: Too many refusals
- Caution: Failure on item 75-90th percentile line
- Delay: Failure on item completely to LEFT of age line (>90th percentile passing)

### Bayley-4 Structure
**[BOARD]**:
- **Cognitive Scale**: Attention, exploration, manipulation, concept formation
- **Language Scale**: Receptive Communication + Expressive Communication
- **Motor Scale**: Fine Motor + Gross Motor
- **Social-Emotional Scale**: Greenspan's Social-Emotional Growth Chart framework
- **Adaptive Behavior Scale**: Communication, Community Use, Health/Safety, Leisure, Self-Care, Self-Direction, Social
- Scoring: Composite scores Mean=100, SD=15; Subtest scaled scores Mean=10, SD=3

### Developmental Quotient (DQ)
**[BOARD]**: DQ = (developmental age / chronological age) x 100
- Used when standardized testing unavailable.
- DQ <70 is roughly equivalent to >2 SD below mean.
- In premature infants, use CORRECTED AGE for denominator until 2 years (some say 3 years for extremely preterm).

## 6. DSM-5 CRITERIA AND CLASSIFICATIONS

### Autism Spectrum Disorder (DSM-5, 2013; DSM-5-TR, 2022)
**[BOARD]** ASD Diagnostic Criteria:

**A. Persistent deficits in social communication and social interaction across multiple contexts** (all 3 required):
1. Deficits in social-emotional reciprocity (abnormal social approach, failure of normal back-and-forth conversation, reduced sharing of interests/emotions/affect, failure to initiate or respond to social interactions)
2. Deficits in nonverbal communicative behaviors (poorly integrated verbal and nonverbal communication, abnormal eye contact and body language, deficits in understanding and use of gestures, total lack of facial expressions and nonverbal communication)
3. Deficits in developing, maintaining, and understanding relationships (difficulty adjusting behavior to social contexts, difficulty sharing imaginative play or making friends, absence of interest in peers)

**B. Restricted, repetitive patterns of behavior, interests, or activities** (at least 2 of 4):
1. Stereotyped or repetitive motor movements, use of objects, or speech (motor stereotypies, echolalia, lining up toys, idiosyncratic phrases)
2. Insistence on sameness, inflexible adherence to routines, or ritualized patterns (extreme distress at small changes, rigid thinking patterns, greeting rituals, need to take same route)
3. Highly restricted, fixated interests that are abnormal in intensity or focus (strong attachment to unusual objects, excessively circumscribed or perseverative interests)
4. Hyper- or hypo-reactivity to sensory input or unusual interest in sensory aspects (apparent indifference to pain/temperature, adverse response to specific sounds/textures, excessive smelling/touching, visual fascination with lights/movement)

**C.** Symptoms must be present in the early developmental period (but may not become fully manifest until social demands exceed limited capacities)

**D.** Symptoms cause clinically significant impairment in social, occupational, or other important areas of current functioning

**E.** Disturbances not better explained by intellectual disability or global developmental delay

**[BOARD]** Severity Levels:
- **Level 1**: "Requiring support" -- Without support, deficits cause noticeable impairments
- **Level 2**: "Requiring substantial support" -- Marked deficits, limited initiation, reduced response
- **Level 3**: "Requiring very substantial support" -- Severe deficits, very limited initiation, minimal response

**[BOARD]** Specifiers: With/without intellectual impairment, with/without language impairment, associated with known medical/genetic condition, associated with another neurodevelopmental/mental/behavioral disorder, with catatonia.

**[BOARD]** Key DSM-5 changes from DSM-IV:
- Asperger syndrome, PDD-NOS, and autistic disorder MERGED into single ASD diagnosis.
- Social communication and restricted/repetitive behaviors are TWO domains (was three: social, communication, RRB).
- Added sensory processing symptoms (hyper/hypo-reactivity) as criterion.
- Added severity levels.
- New diagnosis: Social (Pragmatic) Communication Disorder -- for those with social communication deficits WITHOUT restricted/repetitive behaviors.

### Intellectual Disability (DSM-5)
**[BOARD]**:
- Deficits in intellectual functions (IQ ~70 or below, approximately 2 SD below mean)
- Deficits in adaptive functioning (conceptual, social, practical domains)
- Onset during the developmental period
- Severity based on adaptive functioning, NOT IQ score (mild, moderate, severe, profound)

### Global Developmental Delay
**[BOARD]**:
- Diagnosis reserved for individuals under 5 years who fail to meet expected developmental milestones in several areas
- Used when systematic assessment of intellectual functioning is not possible (too young or unable to undergo standardized testing)
- Significant delay in 2 or more developmental domains
- Typically defined as performance >2 SD below mean in 2+ domains
- Requires reassessment over time

### Speech and Language Disorders
**[BOARD]**:
- **Language disorder**: Persistent difficulties in acquisition and use of language across modalities
- **Speech sound disorder**: Difficulty with speech sound production
- **Childhood-onset fluency disorder (stuttering)**: Disturbances in fluency and time patterning of speech
- **Social (pragmatic) communication disorder**: Difficulty with social use of verbal and nonverbal communication -- NEW in DSM-5

## 7. RED FLAGS FOR DEVELOPMENTAL REFERRAL

### Motor Red Flags
**[BOARD]**:
- **No head control** by 4 months
- **Not sitting independently** by 9 months
- **Not pulling to stand** by 12 months
- **Not walking** by 18 months (refer for evaluation; most children walk by 15 months, 97% by 18 months)
- **Not running** by 24 months
- Persistent **fisting** of hands beyond 3 months
- Persistent **head lag** beyond 4 months
- **Hand preference before 18 months** (suggests contralateral hemiparesis) -- **CLASSIC BOARD QUESTION**
- Persistent **toe walking** beyond 3 years (evaluate for CP, tight heel cords, idiopathic)
- Regression of motor milestones at any age
- **W-sitting** (persistent, associated with hip internal rotation, core weakness -- controversial)

### Language Red Flags
**[BOARD]**:
- **No babbling** by 12 months
- **No single words** by 16 months
- **No 2-word phrases** by 24 months
- **No pointing or gestures** by 12 months
- Any **loss of language** at any age (consider ASD, Landau-Kleffner syndrome, Rett syndrome)
- Not following simple commands by 18 months
- Not 50% intelligible by 2 years
- Not 75% intelligible by 3 years
- Not 100% intelligible by 4 years

### ASD Red Flags (Under 2 Years)
**[BOARD]**:
- No social smile by 2 months
- No eye contact / poor eye contact
- No babbling by 12 months
- No gestures (pointing, waving) by 12 months
- No single words by 16 months
- No 2-word spontaneous (not echolalic) phrases by 24 months
- **ANY loss of language or social skills at ANY age**
- No response to name by 12 months
- No shared enjoyment / joint attention by 14 months
- Lack of pretend play by 18 months

## 8. DEVELOPMENTAL DEFINITIONS AND CONCEPTS

### Key Terminology
**[BOARD]**:
- **Developmental delay**: Significant delay in one or more developmental domains; implies potential for catch-up. Typically defined as >25% delay or >1.5-2 SD below mean.
- **Developmental disability**: More permanent condition; implies chronic limitation. Term used after age 5-7 when more certain of permanence.
- **Developmental dissociation**: Different rates of development across domains (e.g., normal motor but delayed language in ASD).
- **Developmental deviance/deviancy**: Skills acquired in non-sequential order (e.g., walking before crawling; echolalia before meaningful speech in ASD).
- **Developmental regression/deterioration**: Loss of previously acquired skills. ALWAYS warrants urgent evaluation. DDx: neurodegenerative disease, Rett syndrome, ASD regression (language loss in ~25-30% of ASD), Landau-Kleffner syndrome, seizure disorder, metabolic disease.
- **Developmental surveillance**: Ongoing process of monitoring development at every visit (vs. screening which is point-in-time standardized testing).
- **Developmental screening**: Standardized tool administered at specific ages (9, 18, 30 months per AAP).
- **Corrected/adjusted age**: Chronological age minus weeks of prematurity. Use until 24 months (some use until 36 months for extreme prematurity).

### IDEA Part C Details
**[BOARD]**:
- Ages: Birth to 3 years
- Services: Provided in "natural environments" (home, daycare, community settings)
- Service plan: **IFSP (Individualized Family Service Plan)** -- family-centered
- Eligibility: State-defined; typically developmental delay >25% or >1.5-2 SD below mean in one or more areas, OR established condition with high probability of delay
- Transition: At age 3, transition to Part B (IEP through school district)
- Services may include: PT, OT, SLP, special instruction, audiology, vision, psychology, social work, assistive technology, service coordination
- **[BOARD]** Key distinction: IFSP is FAMILY-centered (Part C); IEP is CHILD-centered (Part B)
- Part C services: Free to families regardless of income
- Referral: Any concerned party can refer; evaluation within 45 days

### IDEA Part B
**[BOARD]**:
- Ages: 3-21 years
- Services: Through public school system; "free appropriate public education" (FAPE)
- Service plan: **IEP (Individualized Education Program)** -- child/student-centered
- Eligibility: Must qualify under one of 13 disability categories
- **Least restrictive environment (LRE)** principle: Educate with non-disabled peers to maximum extent appropriate
- Related services: PT, OT, SLP, transportation, counseling, etc.
- **Section 504**: Alternative for students who don't qualify for IEP but need accommodations

## 9. BOARD-RELEVANT FACTS (TAGGED)

**[BOARD]** Hand preference before 18 months = pathological, suggests contralateral hemiparesis (CP).

**[BOARD]** Corrected age = chronological age - weeks premature. Use until age 2 (some say 3 for extremely preterm).

**[BOARD]** Social smile = 2 months. This is one of the most frequently tested milestones.

**[BOARD]** Pincer grasp = 9-10 months (inferior at 8-9 months, mature/neat at 10-12 months).

**[BOARD]** First words = 12 months. 2-word phrases = 24 months. These are critical "50% of children achieve by" markers.

**[BOARD]** Walking independently = 12 months (range 9-15 months; 97th percentile = 18 months).

**[BOARD]** Parachute reflex = emerges 6-9 months, NEVER integrates (persists for life). Asymmetry = hemiplegia.

**[BOARD]** Obligatory ATNR = always pathological. Non-obligatory ATNR normal until 4-6 months.

**[BOARD]** Moro reflex = present at birth, integrates by 3-6 months. Asymmetric Moro = brachial plexus injury or clavicle fracture. Persistent Moro = UMN lesion.

**[BOARD]** STNR emerges at 4-6 months (NOT at birth like most other primitive reflexes). Integrates by 8-12 months. Must integrate for quadruped crawling.

**[BOARD]** Landau reflex emerges at 3-4 months. Absent Landau = hypotonia/floppy infant. Integrates by 12-24 months.

**[BOARD]** ASD prevalence = 1 in 36 (CDC 2020 data). Male:Female ~3.8:1.

**[BOARD]** M-CHAT-R/F: 20 items, ages 16-30 months. Score 0-2 = low risk, 3-7 = medium (do follow-up), 8-20 = high risk (refer immediately).

**[BOARD]** AAP screening schedule: Developmental screening at 9, 18, 30 months; ASD screening at 18 and 24 months.

**[BOARD]** Denver II = SCREENING tool (not diagnostic). Bayley = DIAGNOSTIC tool (gold standard).

**[BOARD]** Bayley-4: Ages 1-42 months. Composite mean = 100, SD = 15.

**[BOARD]** IFSP (Part C, birth-3, family-centered, natural environments) vs. IEP (Part B, 3-21, child-centered, school).

**[BOARD]** Global developmental delay = <5 years old, >2 SD below mean in 2+ domains.

**[BOARD]** Intellectual disability severity in DSM-5 is classified by ADAPTIVE FUNCTIONING, not IQ score.

**[BOARD]** Drawing progression: Circle (3yr) -> Cross (4yr) -> Square (4.5yr) -> Triangle (5yr) -> Diamond (6yr).

**[BOARD]** Block tower: 2 blocks at 12 mo, 4 blocks at 18 mo, 6-7 at 24 mo, 9-10 at 36 mo.

**[BOARD]** Speech intelligibility: 50% at 2 years, 75% at 3 years, 100% at 4 years.

**[BOARD]** Stairs: Walks up 2 feet/step at 18-24 months -> Alternates feet upstairs at 3 years -> Alternates feet downstairs at 4 years.

**[BOARD]** One foot balance: 1-2 sec at 3 years, hops on one foot at 4 years, skips at 5 years.

**[BOARD]** Tricycle at 3 years. Bicycle (with training wheels) at 5-6 years.

**[BOARD]** Copies shapes progression: Vertical line (2yr) -> Horizontal line (2.5yr) -> Circle (3yr) -> Cross (4yr) -> Square (4.5yr) -> Triangle (5yr) -> Diamond (6yr).

**[BOARD]** Loss of any developmental milestone at any age warrants urgent evaluation. DDx includes neurodegenerative disease, Rett syndrome, ASD regression, Landau-Kleffner, metabolic disease.

**[BOARD]** In ASD (DSM-5): ALL 3 social communication criteria required + at least 2 of 4 restricted/repetitive behavior criteria.

**[BOARD]** Social (Pragmatic) Communication Disorder = social communication deficits WITHOUT restricted/repetitive behaviors. New in DSM-5. Cannot be diagnosed if ASD criteria are met.
