- **Shonkoff JP, Hauser-Cram P (1987).** "Early intervention for disabled infants and their families: a quantitative analysis." *Pediatrics*. 80(5):650-658.

  - Greater effect sizes for structured programs with parent involvement

  - Landmark study: early educational intervention improved cognitive outcomes
  - Effects persisted into adulthood

- **Guralnick MJ (2011).** "Why early intervention works: a systems perspective." *Infants and Young Children*. 24(1):6-28.

  - Family patterns of interaction as mediating variable

  - Cochrane review: EI programs improve cognitive outcomes at infant age and preschool age
  - Less clear effect on motor outcomes

## 4. EPIDEMIOLOGY DATA

### 4.1 Early Intervention (Part C)

- **~400,000-430,000 infants and toddlers (0-3)** served under Part C annually (US)
- **~3.4% of the birth-to-3 population** receives Part C services
- **Referral sources:** Primary care (most common), NICU, parents, child care providers
- **Common qualifying conditions:**
  - Prematurity / low birth weight
  - Developmental delay (motor, communication, cognitive)
  - Established conditions (Down syndrome, CP, hearing loss)
  - State-defined at-risk criteria (varies by state)

### 4.2 Special Education (Part B)

- **~7.5 million students ages 3-21** served under IDEA Part B
- **~15% of all public school students** receive special education services
- **Disability categories (IDEA):**
  - Specific Learning Disability: ~33%
  - Speech or Language Impairment: ~19%
  - Other Health Impairment (includes ADHD): ~15%
  - Autism: ~12% (rapidly increasing)
  - Intellectual Disability: ~6%
  - Emotional Disturbance: ~5%
  - Multiple Disabilities: ~2%
  - Developmental Delay: ~7% (ages 3-9 only)
  - Orthopedic Impairment: ~0.7%
  - Traumatic Brain Injury: ~0.4%

### 4.3 Transition Outcomes

- **~65-70% of youth with disabilities** graduate from high school (vs ~85% general population)
- **Employment rate for young adults with disabilities:** ~34% employed within 4 years of leaving high school
- **Post-secondary education:** ~34% of youth with disabilities attend some post-secondary education
- **Independent living:** Variable by disability; ~15-25% of adults with intellectual disability live independently

### 4.4 Lifespan Data

#### CP Lifespan Issues
- **Life expectancy:** GMFCS I-III: near-normal; GMFCS IV-V: reduced (50-60% survive to age 30-40)
- **Neck pain:** MOST COMMON musculoskeletal complaint in adults with CP (Board pearl)
  - Prevalence: 60-80% report chronic pain; neck > back > hip
  - Due to: abnormal posture, muscle imbalances, degenerative changes, wheelchair use
- **Functional decline:** ~25-40% of ambulatory adults with CP report declining walking ability by age 30-40
- **Fatigue:** Present in >50% of adults with CP
- **Depression:** 2-3x general population rate

#### Spina Bifida Lifespan Issues
- **Life expectancy:** ~70-80% survive to adulthood with current care
- **Mortality ages 5-30:** **~40% due to urologic complications** (Board pearl)
  - Renal failure from chronic reflux, recurrent UTIs, neurogenic bladder management failures
  - Shunt malfunction/infection is second major cause of mortality
- **Latex allergy:** ~40-70% prevalence in spina bifida (Board classic)
- **Obesity:** >50% of adults with spina bifida are obese
- **Tethered cord:** Recurrent in ~15-30% after initial repair; symptoms include pain, progressive scoliosis, changing motor/bladder function

#### DMD Lifespan Issues
- **Life expectancy:** Now extending into 30s with ventilatory and cardiac support (historically death in late teens-early 20s)
- **Cardiac:** Cardiomyopathy universal by age 18; leading cause of death
  - ACE inhibitors/ARBs started at diagnosis or first sign of dysfunction
  - Corticosteroids provide some cardioprotection
- **Respiratory:** Declining FVC; nocturnal hypoventilation → BiPAP → tracheostomy ventilation
  - FVC <1L predicts need for ventilatory support
  - Cough assist devices (mechanical insufflation-exsufflation)
- **Loss of ambulation:** ~10-14 years (with corticosteroids)
- **Scoliosis:** >90% after loss of ambulation; spinal fusion often performed
- **Fractures:** Common due to osteoporosis + corticosteroid use + falls

### 4.5 Vocational Predictors in CP (Board High-Yield)

**Four key predictors of vocational/employment outcome in CP:**
1. **IQ / Cognitive ability** — strongest predictor
2. **Ambulation status** — community ambulation correlates with employment
3. **Speech / Communication ability** — intelligible speech predicts better outcomes
4. **Hand function / Manual ability** — MACS level; fine motor for workplace tasks

- **Citation:** Michelsen SI, et al. (2006). *Dev Med Child Neurol*. 48(8):643-649. Also: Huang IC, et al. (2013). *J Rehabil*. 79(3):7-13.
- **Board pearl:** IQ is the SINGLE STRONGEST predictor of employment in CP; followed by ambulation, speech, and hand function

## 5. CLINICAL SCALES & FRAMEWORKS

### 5.1 Got Transition 6 Core Elements of Health Care Transition

- **Citation:** Got Transition / Center for Health Care Transition Improvement. GotTransition.org. White PH, Cooley WC, et al. *Pediatrics*. 2018;142(5):e20182587.

**The 6 Core Elements (Board Must-Know):**

| Core Element | Age Range | Description |
|-------------|-----------|-------------|
| **1. Transition Policy** | 12-14 | Establish office transition policy; educate youth/family about transition process |
| **2. Transition Tracking & Monitoring** | 14-18 | Track progress; use transition readiness assessment tools |
| **3. Transition Readiness** | 14-18 | Assess readiness; educate youth on self-management, self-advocacy |
| **4. Transition Planning** | 14-18 | Develop transition plan; identify adult providers; create medical summary |
| **5. Transfer of Care** | 18-21 | Complete transfer to adult provider; send medical records; communicate with adult provider |
| **6. Transfer Completion** | 18-26 | Confirm integration into adult care; obtain feedback |

- **Board pearl:** Transition planning should begin at age 12-14 (not 18); systematic approach with tracking and readiness assessment

### 5.2 IDEA Part C Framework (Ages 0-3)

| Feature | IDEA Part C |
|---------|------------|
| **Age range** | Birth to 3 years (0-36 months) |
| **Document** | **IFSP** (Individualized Family Service Plan) |
| **Focus** | **Family-centered**; the family is the unit of service |
| **Setting** | **Natural environments** (home, daycare, community settings) — Board pearl |
| **Eligibility** | Developmental delay (state-defined criteria) OR established condition (diagnosed condition with high probability of delay) OR at-risk (some states) |
| **Services** | Speech therapy, PT, OT, special instruction, audiology, vision, nutrition, social work, service coordination |
| **Service coordinator** | Required; coordinates all services; single point of contact for family |
| **Family involvement** | Family outcomes and priorities are central to IFSP |
| **Cost** | Free evaluation/assessment; states may charge sliding-scale fees for services |
| **Lead agency** | Varies by state (health dept, education dept, or DD agency) |
| **Timeline** | Evaluation within 45 days of referral; IFSP developed within 45 days |
| **Review** | Every 6 months (interim); annually (full review) |
| **Transition to Part B** | Transition planning begins at age 2.5 (at least 90 days before 3rd birthday) |

- **Board pearl:** Part C uses IFSP, NOT IEP; family-centered, natural environments, birth to 3

### 5.3 IDEA Part B Framework (Ages 3-21)

| Feature | IDEA Part B |
|---------|------------|
| **Age range** | 3-21 years (3 through 21st birthday) |
| **Document** | **IEP** (Individualized Education Program) |
| **Focus** | **Child/student-centered**; education-focused |
| **Setting** | **Least Restrictive Environment (LRE)** — Board pearl |
| **Eligibility** | Must meet criteria for 1 of 13 disability categories AND require specially designed instruction |
| **13 categories** | Autism, deaf-blindness, deafness, emotional disturbance, hearing impairment, intellectual disability, multiple disabilities, orthopedic impairment, other health impairment, specific learning disability, speech or language impairment, traumatic brain injury, visual impairment |
| **Services** | Special education + related services (PT, OT, SLP, counseling, transportation, etc.) |
| **Related services** | Supportive services required for child to benefit from special education |
| **Team** | Parent, regular ed teacher, special ed teacher, LEA representative, evaluation specialist, others as needed |
| **Transition** | Transition planning BEGINS BY AGE 16 (IDEA requires; some states by age 14) |
| **IEP content** | Present levels, annual goals, services, placement, modifications, assessment participation, transition plan (age 16+) |
| **Review** | Annually (IEP review); re-evaluation every 3 years |
| **Free** | FAPE — Free Appropriate Public Education (guaranteed right) |

- **Board pearl:** Part B requires LRE — children must be educated with non-disabled peers to the maximum extent appropriate; removal from general education only when supplementary aids/services cannot achieve satisfactory results
- **Board pearl:** Related services in IDEA Part B are services needed for the child to BENEFIT from special education (not medically necessary; educationally necessary)

### 5.4 IDEA Part C vs Part B Comparison (Board Classic Table)

| Feature | Part C (0-3 years) | Part B (3-21 years) |
|---------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Plan document** | IFSP | IEP |
| **Focus** | Family-centered | Student/child-centered |
| **Setting** | Natural environments | Least Restrictive Environment (LRE) |
| **Unit of service** | Family | Child |
| **Review frequency** | 6 months / annually | Annually / 3-year re-eval |
| **Lead agency** | Varies by state | State education agency (SEA) |
| **Transition** | To Part B at age 3 | To adult services at age 21 (or graduation) |
| **Cost** | May have family cost share | Free (FAPE) |
| **Mandate** | Federal mandate (all states) | Federal mandate (all states) |

### 5.5 Section 504 Plan vs IEP (Board Comparison)

| Feature | Section 504 Plan | IEP (IDEA Part B) |
|---------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Law** | Section 504 of Rehabilitation Act (1973) | IDEA (2004) |
| **Type of law** | Civil rights / anti-discrimination | Education entitlement |
| **Eligibility** | Physical or mental impairment that substantially limits one or more major life activities | Must meet 1 of 13 disability categories AND need specially designed instruction |
| **Broader/narrower** | **BROADER** (more students qualify) | Narrower (more specific criteria) |
| **Services** | Accommodations and modifications | Specially designed instruction + related services |
| **Examples** | Diabetes: blood sugar monitoring accommodations; ADHD: preferential seating, extended time | CP: adapted PE, OT, PT, modified curriculum |
| **Written plan** | 504 plan (less formal) | IEP (formal, detailed document) |
| **Procedural safeguards** | Less extensive | Extensive (due process, mediation) |
| **Funding** | No additional federal funding | Federal IDEA funding |
| **Free** | Yes (part of FAPE under 504) | Yes (FAPE under IDEA) |

- **Board pearl:** A child who does NOT qualify for an IEP may still qualify for a 504 plan (504 is BROADER); every child with an IEP is automatically protected under 504 but not vice versa
- **Board pearl:** Common 504 plan examples: diabetes, epilepsy, ADHD (without need for special education), food allergies, asthma

### 5.6 ADA (Americans with Disabilities Act)

- **PL 101-336 (1990), amended 2008 (ADAAA)**
- **Title I:** Employment (employers with 15+ employees)
  - Reasonable accommodations required
  - Cannot discriminate in hiring, firing, advancement
- **Title II:** State and local government services (public entities)
  - Includes public schools, public transportation
- **Title III:** Public accommodations (private entities)
  - Restaurants, hotels, theaters, doctors' offices, stores
  - Architectural accessibility requirements
- **Title IV:** Telecommunications
  - Relay services for deaf/hard of hearing
- **Title V:** Miscellaneous provisions
- **Board pearl:** ADA is BROADER than IDEA — applies to all settings (employment, public, private), not just education; complements IDEA and Section 504

### 5.7 Medical Home Model (AAP)

- **Definition:** Primary care that is accessible, continuous, comprehensive, family-centered, coordinated, compassionate, and culturally effective
- **Key principles:**
  - Physician-directed, team-based care
  - Whole-child approach
  - Care coordination across settings
  - Quality improvement
  - Enhanced access (same-day, after-hours)
- **Board pearl:** Medical home is NOT a place — it is a model of delivering primary care; the physician partners with family to ensure all needs are met
- **Relevance to rehab:** Ensures children with disabilities receive coordinated care across medical, educational, and community services

## 6. BOARD-RELEVANT FACTS — HIGH-YIELD

### 6.1 IDEA Key Concepts

#### Free Appropriate Public Education (FAPE)
- Guaranteed under IDEA Part B
- "Appropriate" defined by Supreme Court in Board of Education v. Rowley (1982): must provide "some educational benefit"
- Updated by Endrew F. v. Douglas County School District (2017): must be "reasonably calculated to enable a child to make progress appropriate in light of the child's circumstances"
- **Board pearl:** FAPE does not mean "best" education — it means "appropriate" education; the Endrew F. decision raised the standard from "merely more than de minimis" to "meaningful progress"

#### Least Restrictive Environment (LRE)
- Children with disabilities must be educated WITH non-disabled peers to the MAXIMUM EXTENT APPROPRIATE
- Continuum of placements: general education → resource room → self-contained classroom → separate school → homebound → hospital/institution
- **Board pearl:** LRE is the IDEA principle that drives inclusion; removal from general education only when supplementary aids and services cannot achieve satisfactory results in the general education setting

#### Related Services Under IDEA
- Defined by law: services required for the child to benefit from special education
- Includes: PT, OT, SLP, audiology, psychological services, social work, school nursing, counseling, transportation, recreation, assistive technology, orientation and mobility
- **Board pearl:** Medical services under IDEA are limited to DIAGNOSTIC purposes only; ongoing medical treatment is NOT a related service (exception: school nursing services for medically fragile students, e.g., catheterization, suctioning)
- **Cedar Rapids v. Garret F. (1999):** If a service can be provided by a nurse (not a physician), it is a related service under IDEA

#### Assistive Technology Under IDEA
- Both devices and services must be considered for every IEP
- Device: any item that increases/maintains/improves functional capability
- Service: any service that helps child select, acquire, or use AT device
- **Board pearl:** AT must be considered for every IEP; if AT is needed for FAPE, the school must provide it at no cost

### 6.2 Transition Planning Under IDEA

- **Age 16 (federal minimum; some states age 14):** IEP must include:
  - Appropriate measurable postsecondary goals (education/training, employment, independent living as appropriate)
  - Transition services needed to reach those goals
  - Course of study
  - Interagency involvement (VR, community agencies)
- **Student must be INVITED to IEP meeting** when transition is discussed
- **Age of majority:** At age 18 (or state-defined), rights transfer to student (unless guardianship)
- **Summary of Performance (SOP):** Must be provided when student exits IDEA eligibility (graduation or aging out)

### 6.3 Vocational Rehabilitation (VR) — WIOA

- **Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act (WIOA, 2014):** PL 113-128
- **VR services:** State-federal partnership; federally funded, state-administered
- **Eligibility:** Individual with a disability that constitutes or results in a substantial impediment to employment; requires VR services for employment outcome
- **Order of selection:** When resources are limited, most significantly disabled served first

#### 5 Required Pre-Employment Transition Services (WIOA)
1. Job exploration counseling
2. Work-based learning experiences
3. Counseling on post-secondary enrollment opportunities
4. Workplace readiness training (social skills, independent living)
5. Instruction in self-advocacy

- **Board pearl:** WIOA mandates VR agencies set aside 15% of federal funds for pre-employment transition services for students with disabilities (ages 14-21)

### 6.4 SSI / SSDI Eligibility

#### Supplemental Security Income (SSI) — Children
- **Means-tested** (family income/resources must be below threshold)
- **Disability criteria:** Marked and severe functional limitations expected to last >12 months (or result in death)
- **Functional assessment domains:** Acquiring and using information, attending and completing tasks, interacting with others, moving about and manipulating objects, caring for yourself, health and physical well-being
- **Age 18 redetermination:** At age 18, child is re-evaluated using ADULT disability criteria (many lose SSI eligibility)
- **Board pearl:** SSI for children requires BOTH disability AND financial need; at age 18, adult criteria applied — only individual's income/resources count (not family's)

#### SSDI (Disabled Adult Child / DAC Benefits)
- Available to adult children who became disabled BEFORE age 22
- Based on parent's Social Security earnings record (parent must be retired, disabled, or deceased)
- NOT means-tested (unlike SSI)
- **Board pearl:** DAC benefits allow adults with childhood-onset disability to receive SSDI benefits on a parent's work record; important for those who cannot work enough to establish their own SSDI eligibility

### 6.5 Guardianship vs Supported Decision-Making

#### Guardianship
- **Full (plenary) guardianship:** Court-appointed guardian makes ALL decisions (medical, financial, legal, residential)
- **Limited guardianship:** Guardian has authority only in specified areas
- **Consequences:** Person loses legal right to make own decisions; loss of autonomy
- **Process:** Court proceeding; requires determination of incapacity
- **Board pearl:** Guardianship is the MOST RESTRICTIVE option; should be last resort

#### Supported Decision-Making (SDM)
- **Definition:** Individual retains legal decision-making authority but designates supporters to help understand, make, and communicate decisions
- **Key difference from guardianship:** Individual retains ALL legal rights
- **Board pearl:** SDM is the LEAST RESTRICTIVE alternative to guardianship; growing legal recognition (Texas first to pass SDM legislation, 2015; now 15+ states)
- **Supporters may help with:** Understanding information, weighing options, communicating decisions
- **Supporters may NOT:** Make decisions FOR the person or override their choices

#### Continuum of Decision-Making Supports (least to most restrictive)
1. **Informal supports** (natural supports, family assistance)
2. **Supported Decision-Making** (formal agreement, individual retains rights)
3. **Power of Attorney** (individual grants authority; can revoke)
4. **Representative Payee** (manages Social Security benefits only)
5. **Health Care Proxy / Advance Directive** (medical decisions)
6. **Conservatorship** (financial decisions only, in some states)
7. **Limited Guardianship** (specific areas only)
8. **Full (Plenary) Guardianship** (all decisions; most restrictive)

### 6.6 Post-Secondary Education

- **Think College (thinkcollege.net):** National coordinating center for inclusive higher education
  - 300+ programs nationwide for students with intellectual disability
  - **HEOA (Higher Education Opportunity Act, 2008):** Created Comprehensive Transition and Postsecondary (CTP) programs
  - Students in approved CTP programs can receive federal financial aid (Pell Grants, Federal Work-Study)
- **Types of programs:**
  - Inclusive individual support model (fully included in college courses)
  - Mixed/hybrid model (combination of inclusive and specialized courses)
  - Substantially separate model (primarily separate courses)
- **Board pearl:** Post-secondary education options exist for students with intellectual disability; federally funded; associated with better employment and independent living outcomes

### 6.7 Aging with Childhood Disability — Detailed

#### CP — Adult Issues (Board High-Yield)
- **Neck pain:** MOST COMMON musculoskeletal complaint (60-80% of adults)
  - Mechanism: abnormal posturing, cervical degenerative disease, wheelchair use
  - Cervical myelopathy may develop (especially in dyskinetic CP with cervical dystonia)
- **Chronic pain:** 50-75% of adults with CP report chronic pain
  - Back pain, hip pain (from subluxation/OA), knee pain
- **Fatigue:** >50% prevalence; contributes to functional decline
- **Functional decline:** 25-40% of ambulatory adults report declining walking ability by ages 30-40
  - May lose community ambulation → use wheelchair
  - Excessive energy expenditure throughout childhood/adolescence → premature "aging"
- **Depression:** 2-3x general population rate
- **Osteoporosis:** Increased fracture risk; especially in non-ambulatory individuals
- **Dysphagia:** May worsen with aging; aspiration pneumonia risk
- **Dental issues:** Increased caries, gingival disease (especially with phenytoin use)
- **Cervical stenosis/myelopathy:** Accelerated cervical degeneration

#### Spina Bifida — Adult Issues (Board High-Yield)
- **Urologic mortality:** **~40% of deaths between ages 5-30 are due to urologic/renal complications** (Board classic)
  - Chronic renal failure from reflux, hydronephrosis, recurrent pyelonephritis
  - Importance of lifelong urologic monitoring
  - Clean intermittent catheterization (CIC) compliance
- **Shunt complications:** 2nd major cause of mortality; shunt infection, malfunction
- **Latex allergy:** 40-70% prevalence; due to repeated surgical/catheterization exposures
  - Latex precautions required for all surgical/medical procedures
- **Obesity:** >50% prevalence; multifactorial (reduced mobility, lower metabolic rate, poor dietary habits)
- **Pressure injuries:** Due to insensate skin below lesion level; lifelong skin surveillance
- **Tethered cord:** Can recur; symptoms include pain, progressive weakness, scoliosis progression, changing bladder function
- **Chiari II malformation:** Present in >95% with myelomeningocele; may become symptomatic (brainstem compression, syringomyelia)
- **Sexual health and fertility:**
  - Males: ~80% have erectile dysfunction; most infertile
  - Females: fertility generally preserved; high-risk pregnancies (latex allergy, shunt management, anesthesia for insensate regions)

#### DMD — Adult Issues (Board High-Yield)
- **Cardiac:** Cardiomyopathy is UNIVERSAL by age 18; LEADING CAUSE OF DEATH
  - Dilated cardiomyopathy; progressive
  - ACE inhibitors/ARBs: start at diagnosis of cardiac dysfunction (or prophylactically in some centers)
  - Beta-blockers may be added
  - Cardiac transplantation increasingly considered
- **Respiratory:** Progressive decline in FVC
  - FVC <50% predicted: initiate nocturnal ventilation (BiPAP)
  - FVC <30% or daytime hypercapnia: continuous ventilation
  - Cough assist (MI-E): peak cough flow <270 L/min triggers intervention
  - With ventilatory support: survival extending into 30s-40s
- **Loss of ambulation:** ~10-14 years with corticosteroids (vs ~10-12 without)
- **Scoliosis:** >90% after loss of ambulation
  - Posterior spinal fusion when curve >20-25 degrees and still growing
  - Must be done before FVC declines too much (surgical risk)
- **Bone health:** Osteoporosis from non-ambulation + corticosteroids; fractures common
  - Bisphosphonates considered
- **GI:** Constipation, gastroparesis, nutritional management, gastrostomy if needed

### 6.8 Key Legal Cases Affecting Special Education (Board Review)

| Case | Year | Key Ruling |
|------|------|-----------|
| **Board of Education v. Rowley** | 1982 | FAPE requires "some educational benefit" (not maximum) |
| **Endrew F. v. Douglas County** | 2017 | FAPE must enable child to make progress "appropriate in light of child's circumstances" (raised standard) |
| **Cedar Rapids v. Garret F.** | 1999 | Services that can be provided by a nurse (not physician) are related services under IDEA |
